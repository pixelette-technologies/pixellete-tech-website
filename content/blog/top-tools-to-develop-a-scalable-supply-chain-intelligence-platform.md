## What is supply chain intelligence? 

Before we move forward, it is important to understand what supply chain intelligence is. It refers to the logical and measured strategies using data analytics and technology for actionable results for smooth working in the business supply chain operation. It doesn’t stop at basic logistics but integrates real-time data from different sources like procurement, inventory levels, warehousing, transportation, and customer demand. The information deduced from these sources is used to drive smarter decisions.

By figuring out the gaps within the business supply network, supply chain intelligence can help businesses handle complex problems and meet strict transparency requirements. 

## Overview of supply chain intelligence software

Supply chain intelligence comes with software that has digital tools, specifically designed to collect, process, and analyze large amounts of data from various sources within the supply chain. By gathering this raw data from procurement, production, to logistics, it turns it into actionable units. The primary goal of supply chain intelligence software is to:

→ Reduce costs
→ Increase visibility 
→ Make profitable decisions

Good and effective supply chain intelligence software provides predictive analytics that helps to track trends and plan for risks beforehand. It also provides cloud-based options that enable teams across different locations to connect and collaborate smoothly. With its effective integration capabilities, the software connects with existing CRM, ERP, and IoT systems, resulting in a strong data ecosystem. 

Integrated software works much better than the standalone tools in terms of efficiency and scalability. Standalone tools work best to provide deep analysis of a specific area, but at times, they require manual syncing with other platforms. Contrary to that, the integrated software provides an overall holistic view of the entire supply chain. 

That is why businesses nowadays prefer integrated software for end-to-end supply chain optimization. Now let’s talk about some of the best key features of scalable supply chain intelligence platforms.

## Top tools for supply chain intelligence development

Among many tools available for supply chain intelligence development, below are the top ones you can use with complete trust.

### 1. SAP Integrated Business Planning (IBP)

This tool is known for its capabilities in supply chain forecasting, demand planning, and integrated data analysis. It combines and integrates data from different departments, and end-to-end planning for the supply chain intelligence development. 

### 2. Oracle Supply Chain Management Cloud

Oracle supply chain management cloud comes with real-time data tracking, predictive analytics, and collaborative tools. It requires a huge onboarding time and costs for businesses that have just started their journey. 

### 3. Microsoft Power BI

Microsoft Power BI is another powerful supply chain intelligence tool that helps to vividly visualise the data. It integrates with Microsoft Power products and seamless dashboards for a user-friendly process. It heavily depends on the quality and the structure of the input data.

### 4. Kinaxis RapidResponse

This tool ensures fast scenario-based planning with end-to-end visibility. Businesses run smoothly with their ability for real-time analytics and stimulating tools. But that being said, this tool’s complex working and unique setup requirements might be discouraging for some businesses. 

### 5. Llamasoft (Coupa)

Llamasoft, which is now a part of Coups, is based on AI-driven analytics and advanced data presentation. This tool works well in detecting and optimizing the risks. However, its user interface is not really simple and requires some technical knowledge beforehand. It might be overwhelming for non-technical users. 

So these were a few of the top tools offering advantages in terms of scalability, user experience, and integration. Choose the one that suits your business needs, goals, and size.

## Supply chain analytics software solutions for scalability

To maintain scalability within the supply chain intelligence, it is required to process and analyze large amounts of data smoothly. And this can only be achieved by opting for the right analytics software. With expansion in business, the supply chain becomes increasingly complex, and that’s where the analytics software is needed. They help in maintaining and organising the historical performance, detecting anomalies and trends, and predicting accurate future outcomes. 

There are a few platforms that are designed in a way that scales with growing operational needs. Advanced platforms like Tableau, Qlik, and SAS are designed for such purposes. Tableau works best with user-friendly dashboards and powerful visualisation features, offering identification of trends and tracking KPIs across multiple supply chain nodes. 

Qlik, on the other hand, provides ways for users to explore data from different angles without any complex issues. SAS, with its strong statistical capabilities, supports data-driven decisions with predictive and prescriptive analytics. If your business aims to future-proof the supply chain intelligence, then these software solutions are ideal as they come with supporting real-time data processing and seamless integration. 

## Implementing a supply chain intelligence platform: Key considerations

It is important to have an understanding of some key considerations for implementing a supply chain intelligence platform, as it requires a strategic and step-by-step approach for valuable outcomes within the business or organization. 

Let’s have a look at a few of the key considerations. 

**1.** Smooth data integration happens to be a vital aspect. This helps to integrate information from various platforms such as ERP and CRM, creating a collaborative data foundation. This enables seamless data flow and end-to-end visibility across various platforms. 

**2. **Businesses should also focus on building a user-friendly dashboard specifically optimized for stakeholders, ensuring that the interface is easy to navigate and actionable points are accessible without technical issues. 

**3.** Train employees to use the platforms effectively. If the employee lacks the skills to act and interpret the data provided by the software, even the most advanced systems would be of no benefit to the business. 

**4.** Data privacy and security should be one of the main concerns while handling customers' or supply chain sensitive information. 

**5.** Businesses should have clear objectives to work on. They should be clear about the specific software they require for reducing lead times, improving demand forecasting, or minimizing costs. Having such clear objectives helps you to find software that supports long-term scalability, adaptability, and measurable business impact.
