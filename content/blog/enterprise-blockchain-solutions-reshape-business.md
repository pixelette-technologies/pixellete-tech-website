## What is enterprise blockchain?

An enterprise blockchain is a tailored blockchain solution designed for a specific business and purpose. Unlike public blockchains, an enterprise blockchain is a permissioned ledger that restricts access, allowing companies to maintain control over their network governance. 

Businesses prefer this approach because it ensures that sensitive data remains confidential, limiting visibility to authorized participants rather than being open like public networks such as Bitcoin.

### Key features of enterprise blockchains

1. **Accountability** → Unlike public blockchain networks, enterprise blockchains adhere to regulatory compliance. Management access is restricted to designated personnel, who are responsible for overseeing all activities within the network.
2. **Permissioned access** → Enterprise blockchains operate on a permissioned model, meaning only authorized users can participate. Access is granted through authentication methods like certificates or digital credentials, ensuring that users can only perform actions approved by the ledger managers.
3. **Scalability** → These networks are designed to accommodate increasing numbers of participants and growing transaction volumes, making them highly scalable for business needs.
4. **Immutability** → Enterprise blockchains maintain a degree of immutability. While public blockchains are entirely immutable, enterprise blockchains offer controlled modification capabilities, ensuring that changes require consensus among authorized users while preserving data integrity.
5. **Security** → Like all blockchain networks, enterprise blockchains prioritize security, offering a highly secure and trustworthy environment for business operations.

By making use of enterprise blockchain solutions, businesses can enhance operational efficiency, safeguard sensitive information and simplify processes. 

## Types of enterprise blockchains

Enterprise blockchain solutions generally fall into two categories: private blockchains and consortium blockchains.

### 1. Private blockchains

Governed by a single authority, private blockchains allow organizations to establish and modify network regulations as needed. These blockchains provide complete control over data and access, making them ideal for businesses that require confidentiality and centralized management.

### 2. Consortium blockchains

Unlike private blockchains, consortium blockchains are managed by a group of two or more organizations that collectively set network rules. Each participant contributes to governance, ensuring a more collaborative yet controlled approach to blockchain implementation.

Enterprises prefer private and consortium blockchains because they offer a centralized yet efficient and cost-effective alternative to public blockchains. While public networks emphasize decentralization, they often lack the level of security and control that businesses require.

Enterprise blockchain solutions provide businesses with strong frameworks for managing transactions and data. By building business using blockchain, companies can enhance security, optimize processes and drive operational efficiency. Key benefits include:

- **Enhanced security & data integrity** → Transactions are validated through a consensus mechanism on a peer-to-peer network, reducing the risk of external attacks or unauthorized modifications. This ensures that no single stakeholder can manipulate the system.
- **Operational efficiency & cost reduction **→ Blockchain technology automates and streamlines processes, reducing manual effort and eliminating intermediaries. This leads to faster transactions, lower costs and improved overall efficiency.
- **Transparency & compliance **→ Enterprise blockchains operate through a centralized yet permissioned ledger, ensuring that all activities are documented and accessible to authorized participants. This enhances regulatory compliance and promotes trust among stakeholders.
- **Smart contracts & automation** → Smart contracts are self-executing agreements that trigger actions automatically when predefined conditions are met. This minimizes human intervention, reduces errors and enhances process reliability.

### Choosing the right enterprise blockchain platform

Selecting the ideal enterprise blockchain platform requires an evaluation of business objectives, payment structures and existing operational processes. Here are some of the most widely adopted platforms:

#### 1. Ethereum Enterprise

Since its launch in 2015, Ethereum has remained a leading choice for decentralized application (dApp) development. It is widely used for financial applications, identity management and smart contract execution.

#### 2. Hyperledger

A permissioned, open-source blockchain framework designed to enhance distributed ledger technology (DLT). It offers a suite of tools and frameworks, making it a preferred solution for industries like supply chain and finance.

#### 3. Quorum

A permissioned version of Ethereum tailored for financial institutions. It supports both private and public smart contracts and is a popular choice for businesses requiring transaction privacy and scalability.

#### 4. Corda

Developed through a consortium of over 300 companies, Corda is an open-source blockchain optimized for finance, digital assets, insurance and healthcare. Written in Kotlin, it integrates smoothly with existing business systems.
